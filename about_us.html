Who We Are - Our Educator


Mariposa Family Day Care is an in-home day care founded in 2018 by Ivy and her daughter Jessica. Ivy and her family immigrated from Hong Kong in 2000. Ivy has been a certified educator for over 15 years and enjoys music, art, and embracing diverse cultures. She often incorporates these interests into her everyday teaching to spark creativity within children. Ivy is also multi-lingual, speaking English, Cantonese and Mandarin. She loves to spend time with her children and grandchildren and enjoys spending time in nature as well as visiting the beach. Mariposa Family Day Care operates under the KIDS Family Day Care agency who awarded Ivy the Educator of the Year Award 2023-2024.

What is Family Day Care?


Family Day Care is an educational service provided within a home or in-venue environment. Family Day Care Educators are highly qualified professionals who are trained specifically to comply with the Early Years Learning Framework implemented by the Department of Education and Training.

At Mariposa Family Day Care we are registered to provide care for a maximum of 4 non-school aged children as per National Law. We also offer after school care and vacation care for a maximum of 3 additional school-aged children under 13 years of age.

Mariposa Family Day Care offers families flexible child care arrangements to suit their needs providing:

  • Flexible hours between 8am-6pm
  • Full-time or Part-time Care
  • Casual Care
  • After School Care
  • School Vacation Care
  • Emergency Care
  • Occasional Care

Mariposa Family Day Care provides a safe, secure and healthy environment for children and their families. We provide a smoke, drug and alcohol free child care environment, undergo regular Workplace Health and Safety training and meet all legislative requirements.

Early Childhood Experiences


We focus on a child's development as a whole through providing stimulating, play-based experiences. This includes:

  • Fine Motor Development: used to increase small muscle development. This may include manipulating puzzles, drawing, colouring, painting, beading and manipulating print materials.
  • Cognitive Development: used in our interactive learning environment. This includes the engagement with peers, adults, materials and activities.
  • Physical Development: used in our interactive environment. This may include running, jumping, climbing, catching and ball playing, and structured outdoor time.
  • Social Development: used to promote active learning experiences. This is achieved through the use of many dramatic play experiences such as pretend play and role playing, games and turn taking and working together on projects such as cooking or craft.
  • Mathematical skills & Critical Thinking: used to increase all developmental areas. This may include phonological awareness, vocabulary knowledge, problem solving, creative planning, number concepts and peer interactions.
  • Emotional Development: used to increase positive self concepts. This may include the importance of human relationships such as peer interactions and adult/child interactions. This also includes peer negotiations, the ability to make choices and how to properly interact with others.
